Multiple Supernovae Explosions

Supernova explosions inject significant energy into the interstellar medium to control the formation of next generation stars.

Low-metallicity Universe

The gas-phase metal abundance in the interstellar medium is a key to control the efficiency to form stars. It was low in the past and I investigate the universality/variety of physics controlling star formation in the ancient Universe.

Turbulence in the Universe

Gas turbulent motions in the Universe control the density structure of the interstellar medium and subsequent star formation.

Thermal Instability

Thermal instability is a phase transition in the interstellar medium, which initiates star formation. The instability also has an physical analogy in wide variety of natural phenomena beyond astrophysics.

Molecular Cloud Evolution

Molecular clouds are the direct progenitor of stars and thus their formation and evolution mechanism is essential to understand how stars form with what efficiency across the Universe.

Gravitational Lensing

Gravitational potential of galaxies magnifies the light from galaxies at a further distance. I propose a new technique to measure mass of the intervening galaxies by measuring such magnified signals in observational data.
